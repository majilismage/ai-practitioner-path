üìã Briefing

You're probably already paying for Microsoft Copilot and barely using it. Or you tried it once, got mediocre results, and wrote it off. That's because most people approach Copilot like a magic wand instead of a power tool.

The truth: Copilot isn't trying to replace you. It's trying to eliminate the tedious parts of your job so you can focus on the strategic work. But it's only as good as your ability to direct it.

Here's what Copilot does exceptionally well: data manipulation, first drafts, format conversions, and pattern recognition. Here's what it struggles with: nuanced judgment, complex analysis that requires domain expertise, and understanding your company's specific context.

The key is knowing which tasks to delegate and how to structure your requests.

üéØ Core

Excel Copilot ‚Äî Your Data Analyst:

What it's great at:

  • Creating pivot tables and charts from raw data
  • Writing complex formulas (XLOOKUP, nested IFs, array formulas)
  • Cleaning and organizing messy datasets
  • Generating insights from numerical data

How to use it effectively:

  • "Create a pivot table showing quarterly sales by region and product category"
  • "Write a formula to calculate the 90-day moving average for column D"
  • "Analyze this customer data and identify the top 5 patterns that correlate with churn"

Where it falls short:

  • Understanding what metrics matter for your business
  • Knowing when data looks wrong or suspicious
  • Contextualizing insights within your industry

PowerPoint Copilot ‚Äî Your Designer:

What it's great at:

  • Converting Word documents or PDFs into slide decks
  • Redesigning slides for better visual hierarchy
  • Creating presenter notes and talking points
  • Generating multiple layout options

How to use it effectively:

  • "Turn this 10-page report into a 5-slide executive summary deck"
  • "Redesign these slides to be more visually engaging for a C-level audience"
  • "Create presenter notes for each slide that include relevant data points"

Where it falls short:

  • Understanding your brand guidelines
  • Knowing what resonates with your specific audience
  • Creating compelling narratives from disconnected information

Word Copilot ‚Äî Your Editor:

What it's great at:

  • Summarizing long documents
  • Rewriting content for different audiences
  • Creating outlines and structures
  • Generating multiple versions of the same content

How to use it effectively:

  • "Summarize this 50-page contract focusing on deliverables and deadlines"
  • "Rewrite this technical proposal for a non-technical audience"
  • "Create 3 different versions of this email: formal, casual, and urgent"

Where it falls short:

  • Capturing your unique voice and style
  • Understanding complex business relationships
  • Making strategic content decisions

Your Assignment:

Choose one of these real work scenarios:

Option A: Data Analysis & Deck Creation

  1. Take a dataset you've been meaning to analyze (sales data, survey results, performance metrics, etc.)
  2. Upload it to Excel and use Copilot to:
    • Clean and organize the data
    • Create 2-3 pivot tables showing different insights
    • Generate charts that highlight key trends
    • Ask: "What are the 3 most important insights from this data?"
  3. Use PowerPoint Copilot to:
    • Create a 5-slide deck from the Excel analysis
    • Add presenter notes
    • Redesign for visual impact

Option B: Report to Deck Conversion

  1. Take a recent report, proposal, or lengthy document
  2. Use Word Copilot to:
    • Create an executive summary
    • Extract key data points and recommendations
  3. Use PowerPoint Copilot to:
    • Convert the summary into a 5-slide presentation
    • Generate 3 different design approaches
    • Create talking points for each slide

Specific Copilot Prompts to Use:

Excel:

  • "Analyze this data and create visualizations that would be compelling for executives"
  • "What questions should I be asking about this dataset?"
  • "Create a summary table with the key metrics and trends"

PowerPoint:

  • "Create a presentation from this content that tells a compelling story"
  • "Redesign these slides for maximum visual impact in a boardroom setting"
  • "Generate presenter notes that include supporting data and potential objections"

Document Everything: Note what worked well, what needed manual intervention, and how much time it saved vs. doing it manually.

The Pattern:

Most people discover that Copilot is exceptional at the "heavy lifting" ‚Äî data manipulation, first drafts, format conversions ‚Äî but still requires human oversight for context, strategy, and final polish.

Strategic Takeaway:

The goal isn't to eliminate your involvement, it's to eliminate the tedious parts so you can focus on the parts that require judgment, creativity, and business understanding.
