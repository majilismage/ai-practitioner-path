Module 4.4

Production AI System Design

Scaling AI agents from prototypes to enterprise-ready systems

Briefing

Moving from experimental AI agents to production systems requires careful attention to reliability, scalability, monitoring, and governance. This module covers the engineering and operational practices needed to deploy AI agents that can handle real business workloads with enterprise-grade reliability.

We'll explore monitoring, error handling, performance optimization, and the operational disciplines needed to maintain AI systems in production environments.

Core

Production Requirements:

Reliability Engineering:

  • Error Handling: Graceful degradation and recovery
  • Retry Logic: Intelligent retry with exponential backoff
  • Circuit Breakers: Protection against cascade failures
  • Health Checks: Continuous system status monitoring

Scalability Considerations:

  • Load Management: Handling varying workloads
  • Resource Optimization: Efficient use of compute and memory
  • Concurrency: Safe parallel processing
  • Rate Limiting: Protecting external services and APIs

Monitoring and Observability:

  • Metrics Collection: Performance and business KPIs
  • Logging Strategy: Comprehensive audit trails
  • Alerting: Proactive issue notification
  • Dashboards: Real-time system visibility

Quality Assurance:

Testing Strategies:

  • Unit Testing: Individual component validation
  • Integration Testing: End-to-end workflow verification
  • Load Testing: Performance under realistic conditions
  • Regression Testing: Ensuring updates don't break existing functionality

Deployment Practices:

  • Blue-Green Deployment: Zero-downtime updates
  • Canary Releases: Gradual rollout with safety monitoring
  • Feature Flags: Safe feature activation and rollback
  • Rollback Procedures: Quick recovery from failed deployments

Governance and Compliance:

Operational Controls:

  • Access Management: Role-based permissions and authentication
  • Audit Trails: Complete record of system actions
  • Change Management: Controlled update processes
  • Incident Response: Structured problem resolution

Business Continuity:

  • Backup Procedures: Data and configuration protection
  • Disaster Recovery: Business continuity planning
  • Maintenance Windows: Scheduled downtime management
  • Support Procedures: Issue escalation and resolution
